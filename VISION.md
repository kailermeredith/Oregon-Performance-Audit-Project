## Why the Oregon Performance Audit Project Exists

Effective government depends on more than sound policies—it depends on whether those policies are implemented, evaluated, and continuously improved.

The Oregon Performance Audit Project (OPAP) was established to promote transparency, accountability, and evidence-based governance by examining how Oregon state agencies respond to recommendations issued through performance audits.

Performance audits represent one of the state's most valuable oversight tools. They identify opportunities to improve efficiency, effectiveness, stewardship of public resources, and service delivery. However, the long-term public value of an audit depends not only on the quality of its findings, but on whether its recommendations are ultimately implemented.

OPAP seeks to better understand the implementation process through systematic, transparent, and reproducible research.

---

## Our Vision

We envision a public sector where evidence informs decision-making, implementation is measured consistently, and government performance can be evaluated openly by policymakers, public servants, researchers, and citizens alike.

By developing standardized methodologies and publishing accessible analyses, OPAP aims to contribute to a culture of continuous improvement across Oregon state government.

---

## Guiding Principles

### Independence

OPAP is conducted independently of any government agency, political party, advocacy organization, or private interest. Research is guided by evidence, transparency, and methodological rigor.

### Objectivity

Analyses are conducted using standardized evaluation criteria designed to minimize subjective interpretation and maximize consistency.

### Transparency

Methodologies, coding procedures, documentation, and research outputs are publicly available whenever practical to encourage reproducibility and public understanding.

### Constructive Evaluation

The purpose of OPAP is not to criticize agencies or individuals, but to identify patterns, document implementation progress, and encourage continuous institutional improvement.

### Continuous Learning

Research methodologies will continue to evolve as additional audits are reviewed, new information becomes available, and lessons are learned throughout the project.

---

## Long-Term Goals

Over time, OPAP seeks to:

- Build a comprehensive inventory of Oregon performance audits.
- Track implementation of audit recommendations using standardized methodologies.
- Publish periodic reports identifying statewide trends.
- Develop openly available research tools and coding frameworks.
- Support evidence-based policymaking through accessible public research.
- Encourage discussion about government performance, transparency, and accountability.

---

## Looking Ahead

The Oregon Performance Audit Project is intended to serve as a long-term research initiative documenting the implementation of Oregon's performance audit recommendations. As the project grows, future work may include expanded datasets, visualizations, longitudinal analyses, comparative studies, and collaboration with researchers and public institutions interested in government performance and public administration. Ultimately, OPAP seeks to make performance audit information more understandable, more accessible, and more useful for everyone interested in improving public governance.
