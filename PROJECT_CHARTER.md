# Project Charter

**Project:** Oregon Performance Audit Project (OPAP)

**Version:** 1.0

**Effective Date:** July 2026

**Status:** Active

---

# Purpose

The Oregon Performance Audit Project (OPAP) is an independent, nonpartisan research initiative dedicated to evaluating the implementation of recommendations issued through performance audits conducted by the Oregon Secretary of State Audits Division.

The project exists to improve public understanding of government performance by producing transparent, evidence-based analyses of audit implementation while promoting methodological rigor, reproducibility, and accessibility.

---

# Mission

To advance government accountability by developing reliable, transparent, and publicly accessible research on the implementation of Oregon performance audit recommendations.

---

# Vision

A public sector where performance audit recommendations are systematically evaluated, implementation is transparent, and evidence informs continuous improvement across government institutions.

---

# Strategic Objectives

The Oregon Performance Audit Project pursues six strategic objectives:

1. Develop a comprehensive inventory of Oregon performance audits.

2. Establish standardized methodologies for evaluating recommendation implementation.

3. Produce reproducible analyses using publicly documented research procedures.

4. Publish accessible reports that communicate findings to policymakers, researchers, and the public.

5. Continuously improve project methodologies through documentation and version control.

6. Maintain political neutrality through evidence-based evaluation.

---

# Scope

The project currently includes:

- Oregon Secretary of State Performance Audits
- Audit recommendations
- Public implementation documentation
- Agency responses
- Follow-up reports
- Publicly available legislative information

Future expansion may include:

- Comparative state studies
- Federal oversight research
- Public-sector performance metrics
- Interactive analytical dashboards

---

# Out of Scope

The project does not:

- Conduct financial audits.
- Investigate misconduct.
- Evaluate political ideology.
- Assess agency performance beyond publicly documented audit implementation.
- Replace official oversight functions.

---

# Governance

The project is directed by the Project Lead.

Responsibilities include:

- Research design
- Methodological development
- Data management
- Quality assurance
- Publication review
- Version management

Major methodological revisions are documented through formal version releases.

---

# Research Standards

OPAP is guided by the following principles:

## Independence

Research remains independent of political parties, advocacy organizations, and government agencies.

## Transparency

Methodologies, documentation, and project updates are publicly available whenever practical.

## Objectivity

Research conclusions are based upon documented evidence using standardized evaluation criteria.

## Reproducibility

Analytical procedures are documented to enable independent review and replication.

## Continuous Improvement

Methodologies are refined as additional audits and implementation experience improve the project.

- Methodological revisions
- Dataset expansions
